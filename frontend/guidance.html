Guidance of the Reporting Process

I'm too embarassed, how could I be so stupid?

  • Don't feel ashamed, scams can target anyone!
  • You may also be more vulnerable during certain time periods, such as finding a job.
  • You are not alone. Scams are the most common crime that occurs in the UK.
  • The social stigma, though, means that they are under reported.

I'm worried I might get in trouble for being a victim

  • It is better to be proactive than to try and hide it, or pretend it never happened.
  • Certain scams, such as Money Mules, could have serious consequences if you are caught.
  • However, the punishments only apply to people who aren't cooperative or truthful.
  • Support on Money Mules

I don't know where to even start?

  • If you get a suspicious call, hang up and call 159!
  • This is a phone number that will connect you to almost any bank within the UK.
  • Learn more about 159
  • Once you've done that, you can contact the following numbers:
  • 101, or report it to the police here.
  • 0300 123 2040, or report it to Action Fraud here.
  • For other forms of support like mental health, the following links and resources may be helpful.
  • Citizens Advice
  • 0300 102 1234, or Mind UK
  • 0800 1111, or Childline (For young people under 19)
